Wisconsin Springs

Select a spring on the map to learn more!

Between 2014 and 2017, springs in Wisconsin were identified, mapped, and surveyed by the Wisconsin Geological and Natural History Survey. The lower flow threshold for inclusion in this inventory was approximately 0.25 ft³/s at the time of measurement. The inventory (available as an ArcGIS REST Service) provides detailed descriptions of 415 springs in 58 counties. It includes information on spring type, flow rate, water quality (pH, temperature, conductivity), ecological integrity (such as the presence of invasive plants, recreational activities, roads, livestock, agriculture, and other disturbances), and geomorphic and geologic information (such as spring dimensions, surface type, substrate composition, and bedrock type).
